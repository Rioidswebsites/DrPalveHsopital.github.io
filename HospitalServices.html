In house pharmacy

A home pharmacy aims to ensure that patients have access to essential medications for self-management of their conditions, particularly after discharge from the hospital. It can help prevent medication errors, improve adherence, and provide a readily available supply of medications in case of emergencies or disruptions in regular pharmacy services.

Spacious general ward

General wards are designed to accommodate patients who require medical care but do not need intensive monitoring or specialized treatment provided in critical care units or specialty wards. They cater to a wide range of medical conditions, including general medicine, surgery, pediatrics, and others.

Diabetes speciality care

Many hospitals have dedicated diabetes clinics or centers staffed by healthcare professionals specialized in managing diabetes. These clinics provide comprehensive care, education, and support to patients with diabetes. They may include endocrinologists, certified diabetes educators (CDEs), dietitians, nurses, and other healthcare professionals.

Dedicated diagnostic center

Diagnostic centers employ highly skilled radiologists, radiologic technologists, and other healthcare professionals who specialize in diagnostic imaging. Radiologists interpret the imaging results and provide detailed reports to referring physicians. The staff is trained in operating the equipment, positioning patients correctly, and ensuring the safety and comfort of patients during the procedures.

Well-Equiped Emergency Department

A well-equipped ED is staffed by a team of highly trained healthcare professionals. This includes emergency physicians, nurses, paramedics, and other support staff who are experienced in handling medical emergencies. These professionals work collaboratively to assess and stabilize patients.
