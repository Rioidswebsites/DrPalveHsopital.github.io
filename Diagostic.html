ABPM (Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring)

During AMBP, a patient wears a portable blood pressure monitor, commonly known as an ambulatory blood pressure monitor, for a predetermined period, typically 24 hours. The monitor is connected to a cuff that is wrapped around the patient's upper arm, and it automatically inflates and deflates to measure blood pressure at regular intervals throughout the day and night.

  • Blood Pressure Variability Assessment
  • Evaluation of Treatment Effectiveness
  • Patient Compliance and Awareness
  • Long-Term Monitoring
CGMS (Continuous Glucose Monitoring System)

CGMS is primarily used by individuals with diabetes to monitor their blood glucose levels continuously. It provides valuable information about glucose fluctuations, trends, and patterns, helping individuals and healthcare professionals make informed decisions about diabetes management, including medication dosing, meal planning, and lifestyle modifications.

  • Real-Time CGMS
  • Integrated CGMS
  • Flash CGMS
  • Wearable CGMS
